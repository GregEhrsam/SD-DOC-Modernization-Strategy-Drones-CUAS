The Staffing & Capacity Crisis

South Dakota faces a dual challenge: high incarceration rates per capita and a chronic shortage of correctional officers (COs). The existing South Dakota State Penitentiary (SDSP) in Sioux Falls operates within a structure built before statehood, creating massive inefficiencies. To become a regional leader, SD DOC must shift from a "manpower-heavy" model to a "technology-multiplied" model.

Operational Bottleneck

Current "blind spots" in legacy architecture require 30% more staffing for perimeter monitoring than modern radial designs utilizing electronic detection.

The Sky Security Layer

Traditional walls stop people; they do not stop drones. The proliferation of consumer UAVs presents the single largest emerging threat to correctional security (contraband delivery). SD DOC can counter this by deploying Counter-UAS (CUAS) for detection and DFR (Drone as First Responder) for mitigation and patrol.

  • 1

    Automated Perimeter Scans

    Autonomous drones fly hourly thermal inspections, detecting fence cuts or heat signatures at night that human eyes miss.

  • 2

    RF Airspace Monitoring

    Passive sensors detect drone control signals up to 5km away, giving COs 2-3 minutes of warning before a contraband drop occurs.

Funding via the Airspace Economy

SD DOC sits on valuable real estate. By defining "Safe Corridors" over state land, Corrections can partner with Commercial AAM (Amazon Prime Air, UPS, Medical Transport). The DFR assets used for security can "double dip" as infrastructure monitoring for the state, creating synergy.
